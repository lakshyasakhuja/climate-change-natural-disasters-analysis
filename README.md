# The Influence of Climate Change on the Frequency and Severity of Natural Disasters

This repository contains my **DATA1001 (Foundations of Data Science)** project from the **University of Sydney (Semester 1)**, created using **R and Quarto**.

This was my **first data science research project**, focused on analysing how climate change contributes to the **increasing frequency, intensity, and impact of natural disasters** worldwide. The project emphasises evidence-based reasoning, structured analysis, and clear scientific communication.

At a high level, this project examines how **human-driven climate change** is reshaping global disaster patterns. Rather than focusing on a single event or region, the analysis takes a **global and long-term perspective**, synthesising findings from climate science and international research bodies.

The project explores:

- Rising global temperatures and their relationship with extreme weather events  
- Trends in floods, cyclones, droughts, and wildfires  
- Scientific reasoning supported by IPCC-aligned findings  
- Environmental, social, and economic consequences of climate-driven disasters  
- The growing need for climate adaptation and resilience strategies
